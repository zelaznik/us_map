2016 Election - Was It A Spoiler?

Donald Trump suprised the country when he won Wisconsin and Michigan. Usually they're solidly blue. At the same time, third party candidates Jill Stein and especially Gary Johnson have drew record votes this year. This interactive map will help you decide draw your own conclusions on Johnson and Stein tipped the election to Trump. When deciding whether a third party candidate acted as a spolier, we need to know three things. First, how many votes the third party candidate received. Second, how big was the vote difference between the two major party candidates. Third, which major party candidate would the third party voter actually perfer, if forced to choose.

This interactive map takes real 2016 election results, and reapportions the third party vote. If the Jill Stein slider is at seventy, that means 70% of her voters would pick Clinton over Trump. Same thing with Gary Johnson. You flip a Trump state to Clinton, that state will turn bright Green. If you flip a Clinton state to Trump, the map of the state will turn bright pink. To revert back to the original 2016 results, set both candidates to 50.
